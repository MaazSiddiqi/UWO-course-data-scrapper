CLASSICS 2480A - ROMAN EMPERORS

Course Description: This course examines the characters, policies, and actions of famous and infamous Roman emperors. It examines the virtues of the best emperors, the depravities of the worst emperors, and how these men are judged, using literary, documentary and archaeological evidence to see how their reputations have evolved over time. Antirequisite(s): The former Classical Studies 2903A (if taken in 2014-15, 2016-17) and the former Classical Studies 2903B (if taken in 2014-15). Extra Information: 2 lecture hours.

CLASSICS 3300F - ANCIENT GREEK & ROMAN SEXUALTY

Course Description: This course is designed to give students insight into ancient Greek and Roman sexuality using the artistic evidence of erotic vase-paintings, sculpture, wall-paintings, and everyday objects in combination with ancient literary sources on sexual themes. Topics examined include phallic symbolism, homosexuality, prostitution, male-to-female lovemaking, hermaphrodites, and transvestism. Extra Information: 3 lecture hours.
