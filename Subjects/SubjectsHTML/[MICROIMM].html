MICROIMM 4750G - INFECT. DISEASE BIOINFORMATICS

Course Description: An overview of concepts and applications of techniques in bioinformatics for the study and clinical/public health management of infectious diseases. Students are introduced to the basic analysis of conventional and next-generation sequence data, principles of maximum likelihood and Bayesian inference, reconstructing epidemic and evolutionary histories, detecting adaptation, and molecular epidemiology. Antirequisite(s): Medical Bioinformatics 4750F/G. Extra Information: 2 lecture hours, 2 laboratory hours. Cross-listed with Medical Bioinformatics 4750F/G.
