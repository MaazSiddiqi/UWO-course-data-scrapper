SPANISH 2102A - MEXICO CITY

Course Description: Examine Mexico City through its history of continuous transformations from Aztec empire to the megalopolis it is today. Identify traces of the various pasts in the city's contemporary urban landscape and daily life, through art, film and literature. Comparisons to other Latin American cities will be drawn. Taught in English. Antirequisite(s): CLC 2129A/B. Extra Information: 3 lecture hours.

SPANISH 4412F - SPANISH IN CONTACT

Course Description: Throughout its history, Spanish has come into contact with many other languages: Arabic in the Iberian Peninsula, indigenous languages in the Americas, and more recently - English. This course examines how such contact has influenced Spanish and the contact languages. Extra Information: 3 lecture hours.
